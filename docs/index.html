Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

StackDaily ("we", "our", "us") respects your privacy. This policy describes how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use the StackDaily mobile application.

Information We Collect: We collect your email address and name during signup for account creation. We store your topic subscriptions, reading history, and streak data to provide personalized learning experiences. We collect Firebase Cloud Messaging tokens to send push notifications. If you sign in with Google, we receive your name and email from your Google account. Payment information is processed securely through Razorpay and is never stored on our servers.

How We Use Your Information: To deliver daily questions based on your subscribed topics. To track your learning streaks and progress. To send push notifications about new daily content. To process Pro subscription payments via Razorpay.

Third-Party Services: We use MongoDB Atlas for data storage, Firebase Cloud Messaging for push notifications, Google AdMob for advertisements (free users only), and Razorpay for payment processing. Each service has its own privacy policy.

Advertisements: Free users see ads powered by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect device identifiers and usage data for ad personalization. Pro subscribers see no ads.

Data Security: Passwords are hashed using bcrypt. Authentication uses JWT tokens. All API communications use HTTPS encryption.

Data Deletion: You can request account deletion by contacting us. Upon deletion, all your personal data will be permanently removed from our systems.

Contact: For privacy concerns, reach us at stackdaily.app@gmail.com
